Welcome to *What's New* — ad hoc notes and updates, half-finished thoughts, and
the occasional idea worth keeping.

To make sure the machinery works, here is Euler's identity inline,
$e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$, and here it is again, given the room it deserves:

$$
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} \, dx = \sqrt{\pi}.
$$

That's the whole post. More to come — with, inevitably, more errors than trials.
